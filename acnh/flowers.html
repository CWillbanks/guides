Generalities

Each flower has a genotype (genes) and a phenotype (color).

A flower's phenotype (color) is determined by its genotype (genes).

Phenotype does not equal Genotype

  • A flower's color is only a consequence of its genes.
  • In most cases, color only cannot tell you the flower's genes.
  • In most cases, color only cannot tell you the offspring's possible genes or colors.

This is why any guide not taking genes into account will be inaccurate.

  • Most combinations will work only if both parents have the right genes.
  • Even if a few combinations might work regardless of parent genes, the chance of producing the desired offspring will still vary greatly depending on the parents genes.

It's advised to avoid any guide not mentioning genes.

Genotype

Genes are four hidden values attached to each flower. Together, they define the flower's genotype, which determines the flower's phenotype. Only Roses use all four genes. Other species only use the first three genes.

Genes have been given an unofficial name and code to make communication and notation easier.

Each individual gene can have three different values, generally defining how much it influences the flower's phenotype.

Gene Notation

As we can see, gene values can be represented in various ways.

  • Numeric notation is how the gene is coded in the genotype-phenotype correspondence table.
  • Binary notation is how the gene is coded in the flower's data.
  • Genetic notation is not used in-game but might be easier to understand and use, notably for people familiar with biology.

Equivalence between 01 and 10 / Xx and xX

Note that 10 is always noted 01 since those two distributions are equivalent.

Similarly, xX is always noted Xx for genetic notation.

Gene 3 / White / W Notation Ambiguity

Gene 3 is sometimes inverted in genetic notation for the sake of conforming to real-life genetic notation norms.

When this is the case, ww is written as WW and WW is written as ww.

Paleh's Advanced Genetics Guide is the main source where this notation is used.

Breeding Algorithm

When two flowers of the same species breed together, their genotypes combine to create the offspring's genotype.

The algorithm used to determine the offspring's genotype is based on a real-life genetic principle called Mendelian Inheritance. Binary or Genetic notations are used here.

  1. Make a copy of each parent's gene X
  2. Cut both copies in half
  3. Choose one random half from each parent
  4. Combine the two halves to create the offspring's gene X

Possible outcomes for the offspring gene may be represented by a simple diagram called Punnett Square.

Growth

Flowers have four different growth stages.

Each day, flowers grow to their next stage if they are planted on grass or dirt.

Flowers will not grow when placed on sand (includes beach and sand path).

  • Trampling a plant reverts it to buds
  • Plucking a plant reverts it to stems

Acquisition

There are three different ways of obtaining flowers.

  • Flowers will be naturally generated when an island is created.
  • Flower seeds can be bought in shops.
  • Flowers can reproduce and spawn new flowers.

Flowers which are naturally generated or grown from seeds have a fixed genotype, which makes them the ideal way for getting into flower breeding.

Wild Flowers

Flowers will be naturally generated when an island is created.

Those flowers will always have their default genotypes.

  • Main Island only generates base color Specialty Flowers on the cliffs upon creation.
  • Mystery Islands will spawn either Village Flowers or Specialty Flowers, usually base color, or fixed hybrid colors in the case of Hybrid Mystery Islands (2% and 3% of happening).

Main Island Cliff Flowers

By the time you are able to reach your Main Island's cliffs, the naturally generated flowers there might have already reproduced because of rain. In this case, you will not be able to know which ones have their default genotypes.

That's why if you want already-grown flowers to use as a replacement for seed flowers, you should not use your Main Island's naturally generated flowers, but use naturally generated flowers from Mystery Islands instead.

Seed Flowers

Flowers can be bought at the Shops in the form of seeds for their base colors.

  • Nooklings Shop always sells your Village and Specialty Flowers.
  • Nooklings Shop's first upgrade, Nook's Cranny, also sells a third flower species randomly chosen from those available during the current month. It rotates every two weeks on Mondays (not confirmed) and will always be different from your Village and Specialty Flowers.
  • Lief sells two flower species randomly chosen from those unavailable during the current month (not confirmed).

Main Species

Each Island has two main flower species.

They define which flower species will spawn naturally, and which flower species will always be available at the Nooklings Shop.

  • Main Island will generate Specialty Flower on its cliffs when created.
  • Mystery Islands will always generate either of the main species.
  • Nooklings Shop will always sell both main species.

An Island's two main flower species will be randomly chosen from the flower species available during the island's creation month and the main player's birth month.

The two main flower species will always be different, even if the creation month and the main player's birth month share common flower species.

Reproduction

Flowers can reproduce, spawning new flowers on adjacent available tiles.

Each day, flowers have a chance of reproducing depending on their growth stage and watered state.

Base Reproduction Chance

Base reproduction chance starts at 5%.

Each flower has a water counter keeping track of the number of days it was watered without reproducing. Starting from a water counter of 4 onwards, the base reproduction chance increases by 5% for each additional day of watering. The water counter is reset to 0 when the flower reproduces or if it is dug up.

Bonus Reproduction Chance

Bonus reproduction chance is added when visitors from other islands water your flowers.

Each flower has a watering visitor counter keeping track of the number of players from other islands who watered it during the current day. Only the 10 first watering visitors of the day will be counted. Visitors who don't water any flower will not count toward this limit. The watering visitor counter is reset to 0 everyday or when the flower is dug up.

Breeding or Replication

When flowers reproduce, they can either breed or duplicate depending on their surroundings.

Reproduction Algorithm

  1. If A is watered, increment its water counter and roll for reproduction.
  2. If successful, search for a random adjacent eligible tile T.
  3. If successful, search for a random adjacent eligible partner B.
  4. If successful, breed with B and produce the offspring C on T. If unsuccessful, replicate and produce the duplicate C on T.
  5. Either way, reset the water counter of all involved flowers and mark them ineligible.

Flags

Flags are hidden information attached to an item.

In the case of flowers, flags hold the flower's genes and various information about its state.

Flags : Raised or Reset

  • Raising a flag can be seen as activating it, giving it a non-zero numeric value or the boolean value True.
  • Resetting a flag can be seen as deactivating it, giving it a zero numeric value or the boolean value False.

Weeds

Weed Spawning

Weeds spawn naturally on islands, as long as their total population is inferior 150.

Each day, a number of new weeds will spawn depending on the current number of weeds on the island.

Weed Growth

Weeds have three growth stages. When they spawn, they are at the first stage.

Each day, weeds have a chance of growing to their next stage depending on their current stage and their watered state.

Weed Spreading

Weeds can also spread, spawning new weeds on adjacent empty tiles, additionally to the default weed spawning.

Each day, weeds have a chance to spread depending on their current growth stage and their watered state.
